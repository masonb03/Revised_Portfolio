01 — Who I am

A bit
about me.

I'm an aspiring frontend developer who believes great interfaces should feel as natural as breathing. I'm drawn to the craft of the web — the way a well-timed animation or a perfectly chosen typeface can change how something feels entirely.

Outside the browser I draw (mostly creatures), play games across every genre, and go deep on prehistoric life. There's something about the scale of dinosaurs — and Godzilla as their cinematic heir — that I find endlessly fascinating.

These aren't separate from my dev work. They shape how I think about composition, movement, and storytelling in UI.

05 — How I work

A small,
slow loop.

  1. i.

    Sketch before code

    Paper, then Figma, then anything that compiles. Cheaper to throw away a sketch than a component.

  2. ii.

    Build the bones

    Semantic HTML, accessible by default, layout figured out before a single color is chosen.

  3. iii.

    Type & rhythm

    Hierarchy lives in type and space. If a page doesn't read in greyscale, color won't save it.

  4. iv.

    Animate with restraint

    Motion should explain something, never decorate. If a transition isn't doing a job, it gets cut.

  5. v.

    Ship, then sit with it

    Use the thing. Notice what feels off. Come back the next morning and fix the obvious.
